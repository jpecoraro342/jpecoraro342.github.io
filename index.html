About

My name is Joseph Pecoraro, and I am a third year Computer Engineering Major at the University of Florida. I love working with all aspects of computers, both hardware and software, but I specialize in software. I am always trying to learn new technologies, and further my knowledge and skills as a software engineer. Recently I have developed a love for mobile applications, and have started work developing both IOS and Android apps.

This past summer I began working at Hatchery Lab, LLC a local Gainesville startup. I am the Lead IOS developer, and have developed various projects while working there. I am also an assistant IOS developer at Bafit, LLC, working on an app called meerchat. All of my projects are currently privately hosted, however be sure to see my portfolio and checkout my github for the latest in my open source projects.

Portfolio

Hey there, I'm currently working on putting my portfolio together. I promise I'll have this section up soon, so you can see all the projects I'm working on. In the meantime, you can view my open source projects on github. All of my work projects, and some of my personal projects are currently privately hosted, so you won't find any of those, but there are a few good highlights on github:

Risky Business

Risky Business is a turn based strategy game for android. It combines the strategic gameplay of Settlers of Catan, and the world conquering military combat of Risk, into a fun easy to play game designed for android tablets. I was on a team of 5 who made this game for our software engineering project in Spring of 2014.

Defend the Herd

Defend the herd won best game at CodeDay Orlando in the summer of 2014. This game was made with Unity 2D and C#, and is a simple fun game, where you get to defend your herd of goats from hungry wolves!

Tron Clone

This tron clone is the first game I made for IOS. I made it using the Cocos2D game library. It is currently broken in the most recent version of Xcode and IOS, but I plan to remake it soon using swift and spritekit! There is a folder in the repo of in game screenshots, so you should definitely look at those.
